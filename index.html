Scotland Mandates Nesting Bricks in All New Buildings to Rescue the Imperilled Swift
New Building Regulations · Edinburgh, Scotland · April 2026
Related Tidings
The North Atlantic right whale population has grown to 384 animals — up 8 from last year — continuing four years of slow recovery after a troubling decline.

Scotland hath distinguished itself as the first nation within the United Kingdom to legally require the installation of swift bricks in all new buildings — small hollow cavities costing scarce thirty pounds each that provide crucial nesting chambers for the swift, that most aerial of birds. The measure addresses a grievous decline: nearly two-thirds of Britain's swift population hath been lost since the mid-1990s.

The swift spends nearly its entire life upon the wing, touching land only to nest, and hath long been denied suitable cavities by modern construction. This simple and elegant remedy — a hollow brick set into the masonry of every new dwelling — requires no special skill to install, yet may prove transformative for a species whose aerial acrobatics have delighted observers for centuries.
